# Scene IX — The Praying Alien: Chain of Thoughts

*Extracted from the master performance script. The section headers
("Chain of Thoughts," "Train of Thoughts Signals," "Drumbeating Heart
Pulse," "Drumbeating Gut Feeling") describe alien-tech mind-reading and
would not plausibly be the alien's own interior monologue. They are
voiced by THE EYEWITNESS as scribed report metadata, then THE PRAYING
ALIEN provides the actual interior content. This avoids the headers
being mistaken for spoken thought.*

*Weave the eyewitness scribings in lightly — they should read as brief
filing stamps, not as competing narration.*

---

### EYEWITNESS — SCENE HEADER
*[Voice: THE EYEWITNESS, brief, scribed.]*
*[Tone: filing the next sub-section of the report. Typewriter bed remains active.]*

"Subject: One Praying Alien."

*[Half-beat — 600 ms.]*

"File: Distracting Chain-of-Thoughts."

*[Pause — 1 second. Camera-cut to interior.]*

---

### SCENE IX — THE PRAYING ALIEN: Chain of Thoughts
*[Voice: THE PRAYING ALIEN]*
*[Acoustic shifts: drier, closer, no reverb. This is inside a mind, not inside a church.]*
*[The transition from EYEWITNESS scribing to this voice should feel like a camera cut.]*

---

### EYEWITNESS — SIGNAL HEADER
*[Voice: THE EYEWITNESS, brief.]*

"Signal one. Train of Thoughts."

*[Half-beat — 500 ms.]*

---

### PRAYING ALIEN — Train of Thoughts

*[Pace: moderate at first, then accelerating. This mind has been triggered.]*

"Without whistleblowing, |
my soft warehouse of logically encoded texts warned me, |
warning of a cosmical joke."

*[A half-beat — the alien notices the sky:]*

"Skies, like flashlights flickering on screens, |
are lit up by unknown and unidentified beings!"

*[Beat — processing. Then the question arrives:]*

"What will the soon to be sworn-in leader of the human-looking USA do |
on the inaugural day |
of moving his gold-plated toilet seat |
back into a whitened office?"

*[Pause. Flat, as if selecting from a menu:]*

"Negotiate."
*[One beat.]*
"Or Deport?"

---

### EYEWITNESS — SIGNAL HEADER
*[Voice: THE EYEWITNESS, brief.]*

"Signal two. Drumbeating Heart Pulse."

*[Half-beat — 500 ms.]*

---

### PRAYING ALIEN — Drumbeating Heart Pulse

*[Pace accelerates — this is a heartbeat. The alien is distressed but trying to process rationally.]*

"Who will be the first |
to stumble upon such a puzzling presidential quiz?"

"Many puzzled aliens have already thought of a clue: |
a lyrical promoter of a supposedly non-ghostwritten book, |
the 'Art of the Deal'."

*[Land "Art of the Deal" flatly — no editorial. The irony is in the delivery's absence of irony.]*

"It is party time |
in a country that is famous for pioneering the hood film genre in cinemas."

*[One beat. Then, private, barely spoken:]*

"For God's sake."

*[Resuming pace:]*

"To foot the bill of our after-party, |
let's recall |
the first spacefaring, nationalized, migrant man |
who thought of bankrolling the chosen chief of USA-based humans |
with green-colored dollar-denominated bills."

*[A pulse of something like excitement — the alien makes a cultural reference:]*

"Let's pop the corn, |
'Boyz n the Hood'!"

---

### EYEWITNESS — SIGNAL HEADER
*[Voice: THE EYEWITNESS, brief.]*

"Signal three. Drumbeating Gut Feeling."

*[Half-beat — 500 ms.]*

---

### PRAYING ALIEN — Drumbeating Gut Feeling

*[Pace slows — gut feelings move through a different layer. This is slower, more uncertain.]*

"Instead, |
as transparent as soundwaves, |
another electronically-spread, intangible source of revenue |
was the gas empowering an updated engine of electoral triumph."

*[The alien pauses, genuinely unsure:]*

"Who knows? |
Just another white lie of mine, |
waiting to be settled |
with the laborious and cheap assistance of a non-human tool, |
mislabeled as a digital bank memory!"

*[A strange associative leap — the alien notices its own distraction:]*

"Why am I thinking of a bank all of a sudden near year-end? |
And not, for example, |
random words like |
'synthetic Tanganyika data lake'?"

*[One full beat. Then, flat resolution:]*

"Because its merits are bankable."

*[Two short observations — the alien reporting market conditions:]*

"Green-looking candles are skyrocketing."
*[Beat.]*
"Green-looking parties are degrowthing."
