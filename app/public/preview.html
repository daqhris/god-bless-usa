God Bless The United States Of Aliens

An AI-voiced performance, rendered scene by scene. Per-scene preview for jury and dev — the visitor-facing player lives at the site root.

Headphones recommended. The piece is directed for intimate, non-looping playback through a single visitor's headphones. This preview page plays each scene as a standalone audio file. The master track at the top concatenates them in playback order — which differs from the script's numbering because THE CHURCH LEADER speaks first and THE CHORUS responds. Each section is labelled with both its playback position and the Roman-numeral scene number from the source script.

Complete Performance

God Bless The United States Of Aliens — Master Track

Fifteen scenes total — the Opening Invocation (Scene 0) plus the fourteen numbered scenes — concatenated with a 3-second gap between each. Produced by npm run render:all-scenes, then compressed by npm run encode:audio.

Available after you run npm run render:all-scenes locally with your Anthropic API key set. The per-scene WAVs will populate the sections below at the same time.

Playback 1 · Scene 0

Opening Invocation

Roman / Vatican-style bell peal (four tolls alternating a "mother bell" and a "daughter bell" a major third higher — 5:4 ratio, brighter and more celebratory than a minor third — ~1.1 s apart, tails phasing through each other) → organ drone swells underneath while the peal is still ringing → THE CHORUS whispers "Someone else…" as a premonition → beat of silence → THE EYEWITNESS names the file ("Alien Report. December 24, 2024. God Bless The United States Of Aliens. An All-Seeing Eyewitness Report.") over a typewriter bed that trails into Scene I. New scene, not in the original script — added to establish both registers (liturgical + surveillance) in the first 15 seconds.

Playback 2 · Scene I

The Eyewitness Report

Voice: THE EYEWITNESS. Bureaucratic, clipped, filing an incident report from outside the atmosphere. The typewriter bed continues from Scene 0 — the same transmission. The PRELUDE that opened this scene in the original script has moved to Scene 0.

Playback 3 · Scene III

Church Leader's Speech

Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER. Warm authority, pastoral irony. First time he speaks to the congregation.

Playback 4 · Scene II

The Chorus (First Appearance)

Voice: THE CHORUS. The congregation responds to the leader's address. Rendered by layering four Kokoro voices (three female + one male) with narrow timing offsets — near-synchronicity. Numbered "II" in the source script even though it plays third, because the chorus was written as the second section; our master moves it to follow the leader's first speech.

Playback 5 · Scene V

Church Leader's Speech (continued)

The preacher is now in rhythm. Pacing picks up, then deliberately slows on "loud siren."

Playback 6 · Scene IV

The Chorus (Second Appearance)

Slightly warmer than the first appearance — the congregation has settled into worship.

Playback 7 · Scene VII

Church Leader's Speech (continued) + [SILENCE]

The longest unbroken passage. Ends with four full seconds of silence. The visitor must sit inside it.

Playback 8 · Scene VI

The Chorus (Third Appearance)

Fuller now. The congregation responds to the absolution with the refrain.

Playback 9 · Scene VIII

Church Leader's Speech (concluded) + [AMEN]

Quiet, almost to oneself. Ends on the only one-word moment of the piece — AMEN, voiced by the full ensemble (priest + four chorus voices + praying alien, six lanes summed). The entire congregation validates what was said.

Playback 10 · Scene IX

The Praying Alien — Distracting Chain-of-Thoughts

Voice: THE PRAYING ALIEN (af_nicole — Kokoro's intimate-headphone voice). Interior monologue, three signals each with its own thin ambient bed: brain-electric synaptic pops with a nervous 1.2 kHz hum (Train of Thoughts), an accelerating cardiac lub-dub with a body-motion chuff underneath (Heart Pulse), and a sub-audio gut rumble at ~42 Hz (Gut Feeling). The section headers ("File: Distracting Chain-of-Thoughts," "Signal one. Train of Thoughts," etc.) are voiced by THE EYEWITNESS as scribed report metadata — these describe alien-tech mind-reading and would not plausibly be the alien's own interior monologue, so the eyewitness files them.

Playback 11 · Scene X

The Chorus (Fourth and Final Appearance)

The alien has rejoined the congregation. The fullest, most inhabited version of the refrain.

Playback 12 · Scene XI

Church Leader's Prayer

Prayer, not sermon. Intimate. The preacher speaks toward something above or beyond. The single word "HUGS" is voiced by the full ensemble, like AMEN — the moment of collective warmth where priest, chorus, and praying alien share a single utterance. The line that follows ("Glory to peacekeeping hugs…") returns to the church leader alone.

Playback 13 · Scene XII

The Chorus (Final Echo)

Slowest of all four appearances. Each phrase a bell tolling.

Playback 14 · Scene XIII

The Blessing

Almost a whisper. Merry Christmas. Happy New Year. Organ drone returns.

Playback 15 · Scene XIV

Coda — The Eyewitness Certification

The filing of incident reports resumes. The surveillance loop opened by Scene I closes here. The typewriter bed returns and trails off into the final 5-second silence. The closing paragraph — the author's ancestral memory of the landlocked African kingdom — is included from the original story text. Ships by default; pass --no-coda to opt out.
