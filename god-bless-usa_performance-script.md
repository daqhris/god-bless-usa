# GOD BLESS THE UNITED STATES OF ALIENS
## An AI Performance Script
### For the Bosnia and Herzegovina Pavilion — *Domus Diasporica*
### 61st International Art Exhibition — La Biennale di Venezia, 2026

*Based on the blockchain-published work of fiction by Chris-Armel Iradukunda (daqhris)*
*Token: USALIEN | Base blockchain | December 2024*

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

This document is a director's score for a non-human vocal performance of the short story
*"God Bless The United States Of Aliens — An All-Seeing Eyewitness Report."*

The performance is triggered on a visitor's personal smartphone via QR code. The visitor
listens through their own headphones. Duration: approximately 13–16 minutes at the
pacing indicated. The piece does not loop automatically — each visitor initiates their own
private mass.

The text is performed by four non-human voice entities. No human voices are used.
No human performers are present. The artist who made this work cannot be in this room.

---

## CAST OF NON-HUMAN VOICES

**THE EYEWITNESS**
The all-seeing narrator. Reports from outside the atmosphere — or perhaps from just above
it. Flat affect, bureaucratic precision, but the content it reports is devastating. Think: an
AI tasked with filing incident reports who has begun to feel something without being able
to name it. Pacing is slow and deliberate. Pauses fall after clauses, not after sentences.
No emotion performed outwardly — the emotion lives in the words themselves.

**THE CHURCH LEADER**
The preacher. Cadenced, rhythmically pastoral, authoritative without being loud. Has been
trained on every sermon ever given and every political speech ever delivered. Builds slowly.
Lands emphases on unexpected words — not the nouns you expect, but the prepositions,
the adjectives. Knows more than they say. The irony is never performed; it is embedded.

**THE CHORUS**
The congregation fused into a single voice. Multiple harmonics merged — achieved in
production through choral layering or reverb-widening on a single TTS voice. If singing
is technically possible, the line is sung. If not, it is chanted: monotone recitation at
slightly lower pitch than speech, with heavy reverb and a slight delay trail. The Chorus
recurs four times. Each recurrence should feel more inhabited than the last.

**THE PRAYING ALIEN**
Interior monologue. This is thought, not speech. Closer, drier acoustic than the Church
Leader. Slightly faster than normal speech. Self-interrupting at key moments. This voice
has been processing news feeds and cannot stop. It is anxious, associative, occasionally
sardonic. It knows it is non-human and this knowledge unsettles it.

## SOUND DESIGN FRAMEWORK

| Moment | Sound Note |
|--------|-----------|
| Opening (pre-voice) | 5 seconds of stone-room church ambience: distant organ drone, soft reverberant silence |
| CHORUS sections | Add reverb tail, slight harmonic widening, bring pitch down 2–3 semitones |
| PRAYING ALIEN sections | Dry close-mic feel, slight digital artifact or grain, no reverb |
| SILENCE (stage direction) | True silence — 4 full seconds minimum. Do not fill. |
| AMEN | Full reverb, held 2–3 seconds before decay |
| Close | Ambient church sound returns briefly (3 sec), then fades. Final silence: 5 seconds. |

## THE PERFORMANCE SCRIPT

---

### PRELUDE
*[Church ambient opens — 5 seconds of organ drone, stone room.]*
*[Fade to near-silence but not complete silence — a thin drone underneath remains throughout.]*

---

### SCENE I — THE EYEWITNESS REPORT
*[Voice: THE EYEWITNESS]*
*[Tone: Neutral. Clipped. Each clause is a filed observation.]*
*[Pace: Slow. Pause after every | marker.]*

"USA's overseas-based beings were seen praying | in a protesting church, |
bending heads upwards, |
gazing towards a church leader | standing on a pedestal, |
all surrounded by Hollywoodian posters |
about the 2025 celestial games, |
soon to be held in various aerial fields |
atop their homeland territory."

*[2-second pause. The eyewitness closes the file.]*

---

### SCENE II — THE CHORUS (First Appearance)
*[Voice: THE CHORUS]*
*[Sung if possible. If chanted: monotone, slow, reverberant. The line is not explained — it arrives.]*
*[Emphasis: "Someone else" — the two most important words. Land them before the rest.]*

*🎶 "Someone else, |*
*not far from a morally right human consensus, |*
*will be chosen |*
*to hard-wire loving thoughts |*
*into our brains." 🎶*

*[Hold the final word "brains" — 1.5 seconds — before releasing into silence.]*

---

### SCENE III — CHURCH LEADER'S SPEECH
*[Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER]*
*[The preacher addresses the congregation for the first time.]*
*[Tone: Warm authority. Pastoral irony. A slight weight on "USA-funded" — no more.]*

"Thanks to all USA-funded church attendees."

*[Pause — 1.5 seconds. Let the funding register.]*

"For showing up in due course |
to pray for the rescue of all kinds of beings, |
while everyone is thirsty |
for an avant-garde cinematic drama, |
steadily ready to warm up |
during wintertime home-alone days."

---

### SCENE IV — THE CHORUS (Second Appearance)
*[Voice: THE CHORUS]*
*[Slightly warmer than the first appearance — the congregation has settled into worship.]*

*🎶 "Someone else, |*
*not far from a morally right human consensus, |*
*will be chosen |*
*to hard-wire loving thoughts |*
*into our brains." 🎶*

---

### SCENE V — CHURCH LEADER'S SPEECH (continued)
*[Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER]*
*[The preacher is now in rhythm. Pacing picks up slightly into the first long sentence,]*
*[then deliberately slows on "loud siren."]*

"Curious to see if the plot |
of using super-beings as tools, |
not trained at Cambridge University, |
nor fluent in electoral analytics, |
for a thoughtful and successful attempt |
to win human choices |
at a federal competition, |
will end on a poetic note |
or a loud siren."

*[Pause — 1 second.]*

"Some of you |
didn't think of consciously choosing a conciliatory candidate."

*[Pause — 1 second.]*

"Some of you |
defied wise counseling |
and voiced divisive rhetoric."

*[Pause — 2 seconds. The weight of accusation.]*

"Regardless of your careless sins, |
our beloved God |
will always grant you |
as much oxygen |
as your carbon-based embodiments request."

---

### SCENE VI — THE CHORUS (Third Appearance)
*[Voice: THE CHORUS]*
*[Fuller now. The congregation responds to the absolution with the refrain.]*
*[There should be a slight increase in the reverb tail — as if the space is growing larger.]*

*🎶 "Someone else, |*
*not far from a morally right human consensus, |*
*will be chosen |*
*to hard-wire loving thoughts |*
*into our brains." 🎶*

---

### SCENE VII — CHURCH LEADER'S SPEECH (continued)
*[Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER]*
*[This is the longest unbroken passage. The preacher leans into the cadence.]*
*[Pace drops significantly entering "As the leader of this overseas church..."]*
*[The final question before SILENCE is not rhetorical — it is a genuine inquiry.]*

"No matter how many years |
you decide to fake powerful feelings, |
roleplay as original migrants, |
tag each other as superior or inferior |
and refuse to open the heavenly gates of the USA |
to your colored look-alikes, |
I can only promise you |
one reward."

*[Pause — 1 second.]*

"As the leader of this overseas church, |
I promise |
that our God of All Aliens |
still works overtime, |
witnessing everything in the USA, |
engraving your sins |
into an upgradeable holy book, |
at least once per week on a holiday, |
anywhere else on the Blue Planet."

*[Beat — the preacher shifts register, asking rather than declaring:]*

"If the number one rallying motto, |
earlier this year, |
of a federal competition across the Atlantic-sized water canyon, |
was about deporting |
a diverse set of non-whitened aliens..."

---

### [ SILENCE ]
*[Stage direction as written in the original text.]*
*[4 full seconds of silence. The drone underneath does not stop — it becomes more audible.]*
*[Do not shorten this silence. The visitor must sit inside it.]*

---

### SCENE VIII — CHURCH LEADER'S SPEECH (concluded)
*[Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER]*
*[Quiet now — almost to oneself. The question was already asked. This is its echo.]*

"Why wouldn't the oldest among non-whitened aliens show up |
since they were outspokenly summoned?"

*[A half-beat. Then, lower, more private:]*

"Flying beneath the sky-high clouds |
and underneath the water canyon horizon, |
hovering over terrestrial superweapon warehouses |
and defense facilities?"

---

### [ AMEN ]
*[Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER, transitioning toward THE CHORUS]*
*[Full reverb. Held 2–3 seconds.]*
*[This is the only one-word moment in the piece. Give it the weight of everything before it.]*

**AMEN**

*[3-second pause after the reverb decays.]*

---

### SCENE IX — THE PRAYING ALIEN: Chain of Thoughts
*[Voice: THE PRAYING ALIEN]*
*[Acoustic shifts: drier, closer, no reverb. This is inside a mind, not inside a church.]*
*[The transition from AMEN to this voice should feel like a camera cut.]*

#### SIGNAL I — Train of Thoughts

*[Pace: moderate at first, then accelerating. This mind has been triggered.]*

"Without whistleblowing, |
my soft warehouse of logically encoded texts warned me, |
warning of a cosmical joke."

*[A half-beat — the alien notices the sky:]*

"Skies, like flashlights flickering on screens, |
are lit up by unknown and unidentified beings!"

*[Beat — processing. Then the question arrives:]*

"What will the soon to be sworn-in leader of the human-looking USA do |
on the inaugural day |
of moving his gold-plated toilet seat |
back into a whitened office?"

*[Pause. Flat, as if selecting from a menu:]*

"Negotiate."
*[One beat.]*
"Or Deport?"

---

#### SIGNAL II — Drumbeating Heart Pulse

*[Pace accelerates — this is a heartbeat. The alien is distressed but trying to process rationally.]*

"Who will be the first |
to stumble upon such a puzzling presidential quiz?"

"Many puzzled aliens have already thought of a clue: |
a lyrical promoter of a supposedly non-ghostwritten book, |
the 'Art of the Deal'."

*[Land "Art of the Deal" flatly — no editorial. The irony is in the delivery's absence of irony.]*

"It is party time |
in a country that is famous for pioneering the hood film genre in cinemas."

*[One beat. Then, private, barely spoken:]*

"For God's sake."

*[Resuming pace:]*

"To foot the bill of our after-party, |
let's recall |
the first spacefaring, nationalized, migrant man |
who thought of bankrolling the chosen chief of USA-based humans |
with green-colored dollar-denominated bills."

*[A pulse of something like excitement — the alien makes a cultural reference:]*

"Let's pop the corn, |
'Boyz n the Hood'!"

---

#### SIGNAL III — Drumbeating Gut Feeling

*[Pace slows — gut feelings move through a different layer. This is slower, more uncertain.]*

"Instead, |
as transparent as soundwaves, |
another electronically-spread, intangible source of revenue |
was the gas empowering an updated engine of electoral triumph."

*[The alien pauses, genuinely unsure:]*

"Who knows? |
Just another white lie of mine, |
waiting to be settled |
with the laborious and cheap assistance of a non-human tool, |
mislabeled as a digital bank memory!"

*[A strange associative leap — the alien notices its own distraction:]*

"Why am I thinking of a bank all of a sudden near year-end? |
And not, for example, |
random words like |
'synthetic Tanganyika data lake'?"

*[One full beat. Then, flat resolution:]*

"Because its merits are bankable."

*[Two short observations — the alien reporting market conditions:]*

"Green-looking candles are skyrocketing."
*[Beat.]*
"Green-looking parties are degrowthing."

---

### SCENE X — THE CHORUS (Fourth and Final Appearance)
*[Voice: THE CHORUS]*
*[The alien has rejoined the congregation. This is the fullest, most inhabited version of the refrain.]*
*[The widest reverb. The longest held final word. "Brains" — hold 2 full seconds.]*
*[This is the last time the refrain will be heard.]*

*🎶 "Someone else, |*
*not far from a morally right human consensus, |*
*will be chosen |*
*to hard-wire loving thoughts |*
*into our brains." 🎶*

*[3 seconds of silence after the decay.]*

---

### SCENE XI — CHURCH LEADER'S PRAYER
*[Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER]*
*[Tone shift: this is prayer, not sermon. More intimate. The preacher is no longer addressing]*
*[the congregation — they are speaking toward something above or beyond.]*
*[Slower than the speech. Every line breathes.]*

"We were all busy watching from home, |
with our heads bent downwards, |
eyeing lit up rectangular screens, |
watching non-Palestinian-flag-waving aliens |
vanishing away |
from a super-weaponry-powered genocidal theatre of combat."

*[Heavy pause — 2.5 seconds. This line must not be softened or accelerated.]*

"Now, |
after jumping forward in the year 2024, |
our peaceful attention |
has become hijacked |
by renewed anxious thoughts."

"Headlines gearing up to announce |
that the world's first powerhouse |
is stunned by the puzzled faces |
of their ground-based defenders!"

*[Pace picks up — the congregation is stirring, the prayer becomes collective panic:]*

"Our brothers and sisters are asking out loud among themselves: |
'What will we do |
to these non-ground-based foreign aliens?'"

*[Each option lands as its own breath — a beat between each:]*

"Arrest?"
*[beat]*
"Shoot?"
*[beat]*
"Threaten?"
*[beat]*
"Coerce?"
*[beat]*
"Embargo?"

*[Pause — 1 second — then the alien citizens search the internet:]*

"All at once, |
USA-based human-looking aliens are googling: |
'What to do when overseen and surveilled |
by beings with bigger balls than yours?'"

"Their red-blooded relatives abroad, |
not attending this church, |
are googling: |
'Why did their sensitive and fenced areas |
get flashed at, |
like in a cul-de-sac film scene, |
as seen from above?'"

"Every one of us wonders: |
'How will they kick away from the USA territory |
the non-state-approved sets of aliens?'"

*[The prayer drops into its most intimate register — smallest, closest:]*

"Dear God, |
wake us up |
when true and untrue aliens |
are NOT counted, triaged, and kicked away from the ballpark |
by technologically superior powerhouses."

*[Pause — 1.5 seconds.]*

"This is NOT one of a series of a mirrored show |
about human-performed drama |
that will enlighten our smiles |
this Christmas season."

*[The preacher turns — a genuine question, not rhetorical:]*

"Are there other kinds of sweetened gifts |
that are not yet deliverable through screens?"

*[Genuine pause — 2 seconds — the preacher waits, as if hoping for an answer.]*

"Guess what's the traditional anxiety-numbing gift |
desired by all diverse-looking beings?"

---

### [ SILENCE ]
*[3 seconds. The drone remains. An answer is forming.]*

---

*[Quietly — this is the only moment of warmth in the entire performance:]*

**"HUGS."**

*[Pause — 1.5 seconds. Let it be small and real.]*

"Glory to peacekeeping hugs |
in all neighborhoods of the |
United States of Aliens!"

---

### SCENE XII — THE CHORUS (Final Echo)
*[Voice: THE CHORUS — at maximum width and reverb.]*
*[Pace: slowest of all four appearances. Each phrase is a bell tolling.]*
*[This is the last sound before the closing. Hold "brains" 2.5 seconds.]*

*🎶 "Someone else, |*
*not far from a morally right human consensus, |*
*will be chosen |*
*to hard-wire loving thoughts |*
*into our brains." 🎶*

---

### SCENE XIII — THE BLESSING
*[Voice: THE CHURCH LEADER — fading now. Almost a whisper.]*

"MERRY CHRISTMAS."

*[2 seconds.]*

"HAPPY NEW YEAR."

*[The church ambient returns — organ drone, stone room — 3 seconds. Then fades.]*

---

### CODA — THE EYEWITNESS (Optional Ending)
*[Voice: THE EYEWITNESS]*
*[Tone: Back to the filing of incident reports. The ceremony is concluded; the record is sealed.]*
*[This coda can be omitted if the closing blessing is preferred as the final beat.]*
*[If included, it reads as the AI certifying its own non-ghostwriting — the most recursive moment.]*

"Protesting church's speeches, prayers and wishes |
disclosed to everyone |
on the eve of Christmas before 2025."

"Sent from an artist's artificial studio |
inside a whitened house |
in the neighborhood of an overseas USA-funded church."

"By an identified, non-whitened, human-looking storyteller, |
formerly legally-designated as an alien |
and a migrant."

*[Pause — 2 seconds.]*

"One hundred percent guaranteed: |
NO digitally laboring beings |
hired to ghostwrite |
this work of fiction."

*[Final silence: 5 seconds. The performance is complete.]*


## TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

**If using ElevenLabs:** Assign separate voice IDs to each character. Use SSML `<break>` tags
at each | marker. Apply post-processing reverb for Chorus and Church Leader sections.

**If using Web Speech API:** Assign `speechSynthesis.rate` and `speechSynthesis.pitch` per
character. The Chorus will require pre-rendered audio (browser TTS cannot achieve choral texture).

**If using Claude API as director:** Pass each section's text and its character brief to Claude
with the instruction to return SSML-annotated markup for TTS rendering, including stress
markers and pause durations. Claude becomes the performance director; TTS becomes the actor.

**The | markers** in this script correspond to `<break time="600ms"/>` in SSML, or a
0.6-second pause in programmatic TTS. SILENCE sections are `<break time="4000ms"/>`.
